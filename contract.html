The Bestie Contract
Year Four — Annual Renewal
Binding Across: England · The Netherlands · The North Sea · Wherever We Both End Up

This agreement, entered into freely and enthusiastically, is made between Tristan (hereinafter "the One Who Sent The Box") and Ann (hereinafter "the Bestie, the Recipient, the one who is hopefully on the call right now"). It constitutes the fourth annual renewal of one of the most important friendships either party has ever had, and shall remain in force indefinitely.

Part I — On the Friendship Itself
I. Both parties acknowledge that what they have found in each other is genuinely rare. Most people spend their whole lives without a friendship that feels this easy, this safe, and this full. We found it. That is not nothing. That is everything.
II. This friendship shall be treated not as a background feature of life, but as something worth showing up for — with calls, with letters, with decorated boxes and absurd Heinz Beans cans, and with the kind of consistent presence that makes someone feel genuinely known.
III. Both parties agree that no amount of distance — geographical, temporal, or circumstantial — constitutes grounds for the diminishment of this friendship. The North Sea is not a problem. It is a setting.
IV. Both parties agree to always pick up where they left off, without explanation, without awkwardness, and without guilt. This friendship has earned that. It always will.
V. Both parties are bound by the principle that ordinary moments are worth celebrating. Not every call needs a reason. Not every message needs a purpose. Existing together, in whatever capacity, is reason enough.
Part II — On Being Good to Each Other
VI. Both parties agree to continue being honest with each other — gently, and with care, but without the kind of dishonesty that would make things easier short-term and worse long-term.
VII. Both parties agree to take each other seriously — not in the humourless sense, but in the sense of genuinely listening, genuinely caring, and never making the other feel small for something they've said or felt.
VIII. Both parties agree to check in. When things are hard — not just when they're fine. A call when one of us is struggling is worth more than ten calls when everything is good.
IX. Both parties agree to allow each other to be proud of themselves, and to hype each other up accordingly, without false modesty. We are both doing well. We are allowed to say so.
Part III — On the Ongoing Traditions
X. The plushie tradition is hereby formally enshrined. The plushie shall continue its twice-yearly journey across the North Sea, indefinitely, until both parties are very old and explaining it to people is deeply funny.
XI. The gift exchange tradition — birthdays and Christmases — shall continue, with full creative licence. The only rule is that it must feel like it was made by someone who knows you well. Which it always does.
XII. Any and all ongoing bits — however long running, however inexplicable to outsiders — are protected under this contract. They may evolve. They may be retired with dignity. They may not be abandoned without ceremony.
XIII. The tradition of long, mostly unproductive calls is not only permitted but encouraged. Time spent doing nothing together is not wasted time. It is some of the best time either of us has.
Part IV — On This Specific Occasion
XIV. The Bestie agrees to open all gifts in the order directed by this site. The Giver has arranged things deliberately. The sequence is part of the gift.
XV. The Bestie is legally required to eat at least one biscuit on this call, in front of the Giver, with appropriate ceremony. No deferrals. No "I'll have it later." The Giver will be watching.
XVI. The Giver reserves the right to feel extremely smug about the decorated box, this website, the Heinz Beans can, and the Dutch language learning. The Bestie hereby agrees, in advance, that all of this is warranted.

Mutual Obligations — Ongoing & Unconditional

  • To show up — in whatever form that takes — year after year
  • To never take for granted how genuinely good this is, or how rare it is
  • To celebrate each other loudly and without embarrassment
  • To always be honest, even when it's harder than not being
  • To keep sending things across the North Sea, as long as there is one
  • To remain the kind of friends who make ordinary life feel like an occasion
  • To remember that this friendship is a choice we keep making — and to keep making it
Signed by the Giver
